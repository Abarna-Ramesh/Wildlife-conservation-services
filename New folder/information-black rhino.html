Species Information

Scientific Name:

Diceros bicornis

Common Name:

Black rhinoceros

Where it lives:

The black rhino lives in Africa

It's natural habitat:

primarily in grasslands, savannahs and tropical bush lands.

Role in ecosystem:

They play an important role in their habitats and in countries like Namibia, rhinos are an important source of income from ecotourism. The protection of black rhinos creates large blocks of land for conservation purposes. This benefits many other species, including elephants.

How can we help:

Don't buy rhino horn products. The illegal trade in rhino horn poses the greatest threat to rhinos today.

Adopt a rhino through WWF

By purchasing certified sustainable palm oil and FSC-certified forest products, retailers and manufacturers help protect Sumatran and Javan rhino habitat by limiting illegal logging and forest conversion. Consumers can help by demanding certified products.
